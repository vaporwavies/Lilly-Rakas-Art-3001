Academic Fatigue

I have been going to college for one year longer than intended.
This, in my opinion, feels like one year too long.
On one hand, I am deeply grateful that I had the opportunity to take things slow
after I became sick in 2018. On the other hand, I desperately need to no longer
be involved in an academic institution for at least a couple years.

I sometimes have trouble juggling the fact that it is currently the most
opportune time that the world has ever known for someone like me to exist;
just a hundred years ago the technology to keep a diabetic person alive was
nonexistent, and the illness was a death sentence.
Likewise, it's the best time possible for a person like me to exist in academia.
Ohio State only began to consider architectural barriers for the disabled in the
1970's, and non-physical barriers for the disabled much more recently.
The reason that this is hard to reconcile is that everything still feels very much
like it sucks.

I blame this on the scam-your-heart-out nature of modern-day America, which
does have its nasty fingers all over our secondary education system, as well
as the absolute lack of social supports in general. However, I doubt that the misery
associated with being disabled would disappear even if society was turned into a
cushy place for disabled people. Being sick doesn't stop sucking even when you're
getting paid.
