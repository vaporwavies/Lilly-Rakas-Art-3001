Extra Blog 1!

When you own rats, it is very, very easy to accidentally collect more rats.

Whenever I see an animal rescue post on Facebook, my brain tends to static
out and I resume consciousness with the animal in hand. I'm only able to beat back the
mesmerization when I don't already have the enclosure for the animal on hand; this means
I'm able to resist the temptation of reptiles, amphibians, and other rodents, because
I'm not able to make space for them with the aquariums and cages I have on hand. But rats?
I can just keep getting rats. Specifically, I can get up to seven of each sex of rat.
Last week, I had four of each. This week, I have five each, for a total of ten. I have
strayed into the treacherous territory of double-digit rat ownership.

I just couldn't help myself. The first one is a standard-coat, black and white hooded male.
He was posted as free on a local reptile group- he was rejected by the person's python and
then grew too big for the snake to eat. There was only one picture, but the thought that
I could prevent this cutie from being choked out and eaten overtook me. And so I got him.
His name is Vinny. He likes to feel safe. He still poops every time you pick him up because
he's not used to guaranteed survival, but after his poops are out, he likes to cuddle.

The second was a female double-rex coat (mostly bald with patchy curly fur), and grey and white.
She was being sold by a small breeder who was clearing out their inventory. She is very itty
bitty and feels like a hot water bottle. She was raised to be a pet, so she's not perpetually
terrified. She's often pushed around by The Jokka, but is learning to stand up for herself.
Her name is Chrysanthemum, after the Kevin Henkes book.

This has been my rat status update.
