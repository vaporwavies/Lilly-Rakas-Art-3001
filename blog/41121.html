Goodbye, OSU

  Five years total. Five years affiliated with Ohio State Arts and Sciences. Three years
affiliated with Art and Technology. Four years of a meme-president that nearly derailed democracy
as we knew it. Came in abled, exited disabled. Mostly, I'm just tired.
  I gained the following skills from zero: photography, 3D modeling, After Effects, a handful of coding languages,
screenprinting, woodworking, robotics, and a host of other things that have ultimately become entirely irrelevant
to my practice. I am much better at animation (stop motion and digital,) figure drawing, and color
theory.
  I feel a touch of bitterness that my last two years were so unorthodox; I never got to have
a standard "senior experience," and I likely never will. In a way, I feel grateful that the
rest of the world is reeling with our new realities, as I feel like it gives me a little bit of
time to get back on my feet.
  I'm reminded of the dungeons and dragons podcast "The Adventure Zone". Paraphrasing:
  Clint: That's a natural 1 minus 1, which equals an initiative of zero.
  Justin: Zero initiative? Reminds me of myself after getting my degree.
  I too am feeling pretty natural-1-minus-1 about the whole prospect of entering myself into the
corporate sphere.
  Maybe I'll try to avoid it indefinitely.
  Who knows!


Thank you very much.
-Lilly Rakas
